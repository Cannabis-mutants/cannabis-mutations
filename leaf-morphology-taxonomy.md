# Cannabis Leaf Morphology & Mutation Taxonomy

**Version 0.1 · CC-BY 4.0 · Cannabis Mutants (https://cannabismutants.com)**

An open reference naming and defining the leaf morphologies and mutations of *Cannabis*.

## The baseline: a normal cannabis leaf
A typical *Cannabis* fan leaf is **palmately compound** — separate leaflets radiating from
one point — usually with an odd number of leaflets (5–9, occasionally 11–13), each long,
narrow and **serrated**. Leaves grow in **decussate phyllotaxy** (opposite pairs, each
rotated 90° from the last), shifting to **alternate** near flowering. Mutations are defined
by how they depart from this baseline.

Each entry: *Also known as · Definition · How to identify · Basis · Heritability & stability · Examples.*

## 1. Leaflet & blade morphology

### Ducksfoot
- **Also known as:** Duck's foot, duckfoot, webbed-leaf cannabis
- **Definition:** Leaflets fused (connate) into a single broad, webbed blade like a duck's foot.
- **How to identify:** Webbed leaflets; rounded hand-print silhouette; present from early growth.
- **Basis:** Single recessive gene affecting leaflet separation.
- **Heritability & stability:** Recessive, seed-stable when homozygous; ~1 in 4 of a carrier × carrier cross (3:1).
- **Examples:** Ducksfoot lines popularised by Australian breeders.

### Australian Bastard Cannabis (ABC)
- **Also known as:** ABC, *Cannabis australis* (informal), Bindi Buds
- **Definition:** Small, narrow, fleshy leaflets with smooth (entire) margins — no teeth.
- **How to identify:** Toothless leaflets; compact habit; barely recognisable as cannabis.
- **Basis:** Recessive; suppresses serration and leaflet expansion.
- **Heritability & stability:** Recessive; ABC leaf reappears in ~1/4 of the F2.
- **Examples:** Reportedly wild-found near Sydney, Australia.

### Freakshow
- **Also known as:** Freak Show, fern-leaf cannabis
- **Definition:** Thin, deeply divided, heavily serrated leaves resembling a fern frond.
- **How to identify:** Wispy, intricate, deeply lobed leaflets; seedlings mistaken for other plants.
- **Basis:** Spontaneous mutation selectively bred to stability.
- **Heritability & stability:** Stabilised true-breeding line; sold as feminized seed.
- **Examples:** Bred by "Shapeshifter" (California); commercialised by Humboldt Seed Company.

### Mnogolistka
- **Also known as:** Many-leaved cannabis (Russian)
- **Definition:** Serrated leaflets overlap and partly fuse, stacking in two rows to form a dense leaf.
- **How to identify:** Doubled/stacked leaflets retaining serration; crowded, layered, stealthy plant.
- **Basis:** Rare, little-documented; Russian (Krasnodar) landrace lineage; basis not formally described.
- **Heritability & stability:** Carried through landrace seed lines; not yet well characterised.
- **Examples:** Mnogolistka landrace and crosses (e.g. Mnogolistka × ABC).

## 2. Phyllotaxy & plant architecture

### Whorled phyllotaxy
- **Also known as:** Tri-leaf, three-leaf, trifoliate
- **Definition:** 3+ leaves per node in a ring instead of an opposite pair, often with an extra branch.
- **How to identify:** Nodes with 3+ leaves/branches; bushier plant, more budding sites.
- **Basis:** Usually somatic (arises in the growing plant).
- **Heritability & stability:** Unstable; does not breed true; often fades into flower.
- **Examples:** Appears spontaneously across many strains.

### Fasciation
- **Also known as:** Cresting, banding
- **Definition:** Abnormal flattening/fusion of a stem or growing tip (ribboned rather than round).
- **How to identify:** Flattened, fan-shaped or fused stem/cola; often one wide flat top.
- **Basis:** Developmental disruption of the growing tip; genetic, hormonal, injury or environmental.
- **Heritability & stability:** Typically not heritable; sporadic.
- **Examples:** Seen occasionally in colas and branches.

### Creeper & vine habit
- **Also known as:** Creeper phenotype; vine cannabis (related)
- **Definition:** Lower branches bow to the ground and grow horizontally, rooting at contact points; vine form grows thin, spiralling, hop-like stems.
- **How to identify:** Sprawling ground-creeping branches, or elongated twining stems.
- **Basis:** Tropical landraces; vine form a rare spin-off of ABC crosses.
- **Heritability & stability:** Rare; not developed into a named strain.
- **Examples:** Large tropical sativas in humid conditions.

## 3. Pigmentation

### Variegation
- **Also known as:** Variegated cannabis, partial albinism, white weed
- **Definition:** Partial chlorophyll loss producing white/pale patches, sometimes half-white leaves.
- **How to identify:** Sharply-bounded white/pale sectors on leaves.
- **Basis:** Usually chimeral (mutated + normal cell lines); generally recessive, often trigger-dependent.
- **Heritability & stability:** Patterning can pass through seed; reduces photosynthesis/yield.
- **Examples:** Occurs across many lines; prized ornamentally.

### Albinism
- **Also known as:** Full albino, white seedling
- **Definition:** Near-total absence of chlorophyll.
- **How to identify:** Wholly white plants/seedlings.
- **Basis:** Chlorophyll production failure; extreme of variegation.
- **Heritability & stability:** Not viable; dies young.
- **Examples:** Rare; brief seedlings.

## 4. Chromosomal

### Polyploidy
- **Also known as:** Tetraploid, triploid, polyploid cannabis
- **Definition:** 3+ chromosome sets instead of two (commonly tetraploid).
- **How to identify:** Larger, thicker leaves; sturdier stems; more vigour.
- **Basis:** Whole-genome duplication; rare in the wild, usually induced (colchicine/oryzalin).
- **Heritability & stability:** Unstable; reverts to diploid/mixoploid.
- **Examples:** Experimental breeding and hemp research.

## 5. Seed & seedling

### Polyembryony
- **Also known as:** Twin seedlings, twin taproot
- **Definition:** One seed with more than one embryo, germinating into two+ seedlings.
- **How to identify:** Two seedlings/taproots from one seed; often separable.
- **Basis:** Multiple embryos in one seed; extras frequently genetically identical.
- **Heritability & stability:** Sporadic; not reliably heritable.
- **Examples:** Occasional across many strains.
